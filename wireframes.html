Redesigning the Undergraduate Course Page to Better Fit the Needs of Informatics Students



In this project we are presented several sample personas that depict the goals and frustrations of UCI students in the B.S Informatics program. Based on these personas, my task was to generate wireframes to help redesign the undergraduate course page to better fit the needs of Informatics students. I begin by brainstorming three wireframe designs before using student feedback sessions to finalize on one and creating an interactive prototype for the wireframe in Axure. (Personas shown in class cannot be shared)

This is the webpage we are tasked with redesigning: Original B.S Informatics Degree Page


Wireframe #1

The design for this wireframe focuses on the personas that identified a lack of information regarding the Informatics major as one of their main frustrations. One of the main concerns these personas had was how the Informatics page failed to provide clear descriptions for the different specializations offered and the general skills that students would learn by pursuing it. One persona stated “Uncertain which specialization will help him the most in his desired career path”. In order to mitigate this issue, the wireframe includes 3 panels that provide detailed information regarding each specialization as well as photos related to the general course work. Personas also mentioned that the page was difficult to navigate which made it tough to quickly find information about classes offered and what to expect from the professors teaching them. In order to alleviate these frustrations, the wireframe design includes a scrollable box that lists the course work, description and professors teaching the course. An image set from each course will also be provided in order to provide a better idea of what kind of work will be done in the class. Several personas highlighted that the Informatics as a degree name can be misleading since it sounds like a major that would be data and analytics oriented. I believe that this wireframe helps provide a better overall representation of what the degree covers.


Wireframe #2

The design for this wireframe focuses on the personas that identified a lack of career direction and opportunities as their greatest frustration. One persona in specific stated one of their main needs was an ”Introduction to different career and field options to incoming students”. In order to help students discover the different areas of work they can pursue, the wireframe includes a sidebar for exploring different career paths in Informatics. Clicking on a career path button will update the right hand section to a scrollable photo set and detailed description of what jobs and skills students will encounter on that path. Another persona stated “inability to find internships and research opportunities” as one of their struggles. In order to mitigate these issues the design incorporates a section for discovering internship and job/research positions. Clicking on a position from the scrollable list on the left will update the information panel on the right to show a picture, position data and link to the application page.


Wireframe #3

This wireframes’ primary focus was on personas that expressed frustrations with UCI not offering enough resources and opportunities to explore the Informatics major and to help form connections. Some personas stated “UCI does not advertise or offer enough opportunities to explore the field” and “lack of clubs and prep sessions for mock interviews”. In an effort to help students discover different skill sets and areas that the degree of Informatics covers, the design includes a section at the bottom to browse through upcoming workshops and events. Clicking on the prev or next buttons will allow the user to cycle through the events and a photo gallery will be included to provide a better idea of what the event will be like. In order to improve the website and help it promote student involvement, the design for the wireframe includes a section for browsing the different Informatics clubs. Clicking on a link will take the user to the club application page. Other personas also expressed a need for “guidance about which informatics courses helped alumni the most during their careers”. To provide students with more confidence in their chosen degree, the wireframe includes a section for users to explore alumni and student profiles where they explain which courses they found most valuable during their time at UCI. Lastly the design incorporates a sidebar layout for the department of informatics page in order to improve usability as many personas complained about the website not being user friendly enough.



Interactive Axure Demo

After conducting user feedback and testing sessions with classmates, I chose to implement wireframe #1 because users expressed it best addressed the core issue students had regarding not being able to find enough immediate info about specializations. Even though the first wireframe design lacked interactivity, students tended to find it more practical as many found that extra modals and carousals brought unnecessary clutter. Since this redesign focuses on the degree landing page, I also decided that interactive elements to explore job postings and career paths was best saved for other sections of the website.This redesign includes a simple carousel to explore the three specializations and scrollable section for exploring individual courses offered by UCI.
